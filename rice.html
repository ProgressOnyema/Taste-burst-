Rice Recipes

Jollof rice

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long grain parboiled rice
  • 2 cups long grain parboiled rice
  • 1.2kg chicken
  • 5 plum tomatoes
  • 3 tatashe peppers
  • 2 onions
  • A medium piece of ginger
  • 1 cup tomato paste
  • ½ cup vegetable oil for frying the tomatoes
  • 2 Knorr cubes
  • 1 habanero pepper (or to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon thyme
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 5 bay leaves (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons liquid smoke

Preparation

  1. Pour the chicken stock and the tomato stew into a sizeable pot and leave to boil.
  2. Add the drained parboiled rice, curry powder, salt, black pepper and liquid smoke and Stir.
  3. The water level should be the same level of the rice.
  4. Cover the pot and leave to cook on low to medium heat.
  5. This way the rice does not burn before the water dries up.
  6. The rice should be done by the time the water is dry.
  7. Taste to confirm.
  8. Stir with a spatula and transfer the rice to a cool contain so it will stop cooking.

Fried rice

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long grain parboiled rice
  • 1 whole hard chicken
  • Vegetable Oil
  • 2 carrots
  • Sweet corn
  • 1 sweet pepper (fresh paprika)
  • ½ cup sweet corn
  • ½ cup green peas
  • 4 hot dogs
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 bulbs of onions
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 tablespoon thyme

Preparation

  1. Precook the rice by adding it to a pot of boiling water and cooking it for 7 minutes maximum.
  2. After 7 minutes, pour into a sieve and quickly cool it down with water and set aside.
  3. Clean and cook the chicken with one onion, seasoning cubes and thyme till done.
  4. The can also add salt at this time. When done, grill the chicken in the oven or deep-fry it till golden.
  5. Pour the chicken stock into another pot, add the curry powder, cover to boil then add the precooked rice and cook till the water dries up.
  6. Transfer the rice to a cool contain so it can cool down and not stuck together.
  7. Next, divide the rice and all the ingredients into the same number of portions.
  8. That is to say, if you divide the rice into 5 portions, divide every other ingredient into 5 portions.
  9. Pour a small amount of vegetable oil in a frying pan or wok, when it heats up, add the diced onions, fry for bit, add the diced hotdogs, fry till brown marks appear.
  10. Add the carrots, sweet corn and green peas, fresh paprika and fry for one minute.
  11. Add the first batch of the cooked rice and fry till every grain of rice gets a good fry.
  12. Transfer to a cool container and fry the next batch. Repeat the process till all the batches are fried.

Coconut rice

Ingredients

  • 420g long grain parboiled rice
  • 400 ml Coconut Milk
  • 400 ml chicken stock/broth
  • 20 small shrimps
  • 1 tatashe pepper
  • 1 onion
  • A handful of diced sausage
  • 1 big seasoning cube
  • Habanero peppers
  • Black pepper
  • Salt
  • Green peas
  • Green beans

Preparation

  1. Pour the chicken stock and coconut milk into a sizeable pot.
  2. Bear in mind that the rice will normally rise by at least one quarter of its quantity from the parboiled state to the done state.
  3. Add the black pepper and seasoning cube.
  4. Cover the pot and start cooking till it boils.
  5. Add the precooked rice, Stir and add salt to taste.
  6. At this point the liquid should be at the same level as the rice.
  7. If not, top it up with water.
  8. If the liquid is much more than the rice, you will end up with soggy coconut rice.
  9. If you used long grain parboiled rice and you parboiled/precooked it correctly, the Coconut Rice will be done by the time the level of water dries up.
  10. Cover the pot and start cooking on medium heat.
  11. Once it boils, add the hard pork sausage.
  12. The hard pork sausage needs to be cooked but if you are using another type of sausage that does not need much cooking, add it in step 6.
  13. When the water dries to exactly the same level as the rice add the shrimps and continue cooking.
  14. When the water is almost dry, add the soft ingredients: onions, green beans and green peas and the tatashe and habanero peppers.
  15. Cover and continue cooking till all the water dry up.
  16. If you parboiled the rice correctly, it should be done by now.
  17. Stir with a spatula till all the ingredients are well combined. That's it!

Native jollof rice

Ingredients

  • 4 cups Long grain parboiled rice
  • 400g tinned banga (palm fruit concentrate)
  • 2 smoked fish (smoked mackerels)
  • 1 big stock cube
  • 1 red onion (red onion recommended)
  • 1 tablespoon ground crayfish
  • 1 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper
  • Habanero pepper (to your taste)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Scent leaves

Preparation

  1. Parboil the long grain parboiled rice.
  2. Pour the parboiled rice into a sieve to drain out all the hot water.
  3. Rinse with cool water to cool it down as quickly as possible.
  4. Leave in the sieve to drain all the water.
  5. Boil some water and use it to mix the banga concentrate.
  6. Slowly decant the mix from step 3 above into the pot that you will use to cook the Banga Rice.
  7. Add stock cube (crushed), crayfish, onions, salt and the ground cayenne pepper.
  8. Cover and leave it to boil.
  9. When it boils, add the parboiled rice.
  10. Stir and make sure the water is at the same level as the rice.
  11. If you parboiled the rice correctly, the Banga Rice will be done by the time this same level of water dries.
  12. Taste for salt and add some if necessary.
  13. Cover and start cooking on medium heat.
  14. When you can no longer see the liquid in the pot but it is not yet dry, add the scent leaves, diced habanero peppers and smoked fish on top.
  15. Cover and continue cooking till all the liquid dry up.
  16. When the water dries, stir very well and turn off the heat.
  17. Leave to stand for 5 minutes before serving.
